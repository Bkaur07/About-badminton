Techniques

Basic Skills


Grip: The right grip in holding the racket is crucial for control and avoiding wrist injuries. There are two main grips: forehand and backhand.
Stance: A stable stance is essential for easier movement. There are three types of stances: attacking, defensive, and net stance.
Footwork: Proper footwork involves moving efficiently around the court, including steps backward, sideward, and forward.
Serve: There are different types of serves, including high serve and low serve.
Smash: A powerful offensive shot that can be executed with forehand, backhand, or jumping smash.

Advanced Skills


Attacking Clear: A powerful offensive shot to beat the opponent.
Advanced Net Shot: Techniques like tumbling net shot, net kill, and net lift to dominate the front area of the court.
Advanced Drop Shot: Techniques like cross-court drop shot and jumping drop shot.

Training Tips


Perfect Your Serves: Work on various types of serves to set the pace of the rally.
Solo Drills: Improve accuracy, coordination, and reaction speed with wall rally drills.
Practice Shuttle Placement: Aim for specific corners of the court to challenge your opponent.
Grip Transitions: Practice transitioning between forehand and backhand grips for better accuracy.
Warm-Up Routine: Develop a warm-up routine to prepare your muscles for action.
